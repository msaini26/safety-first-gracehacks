Safe Havens on campus

Bus Stops

Another safe location to walk towards is the bus stop because there is always strong stree lights and usually more people waiting to take public transportation.

Dining Halls

Locations with more people

When walking back later at night, keep in mind that the dinings halls are usually the most well-lit locations on campus with many people and workers. If you feel that you're being followed, walk towards the closest dining hall.

Night Escort

contact

The Night Safety Escort Service is managed by the UC Santa Cruz Police Department in order to provide students with a safe and reliable means of transportation on campus during the academic year. click this link for more info on this service: https://police.ucsc.edu/services/night-safety-escort-service.html .

Gunshots in area of Heller Dr

IN THE AREA OF HELLER DR AND EMPIRE GRADE/ A MALE SUBJ WEARING A BLACK BASEBALL CAP/BLACK SHIRT AND WHITE PANTS MAY BE ASSOCIATED WITH THE REPORT. PLEASE EXERCISE CAUTION AND AVOID THE AREA UNTIL UCSC POLICES IT IS SAFE. After receiving a report of possible gunshots in the area of Heller Drive and Empire Grade, officers responded to the scene, finding that suspect had left the campus and could not be found. The suspect is a white adult male with long hair, wearing a black baseball hat, a black shirt, and white pants. There is no current threat to the campus community, though the members of the campus community should remain attentive to their surroundings and report anything suspicious. Anyone with more information should call the UC Santa Cruz Police Department at (831) 459-2231 ext. 1. In the event of an emergency, please call 911.

6:46 PM, 17 October, 2022

Weapons violation near Thinmann Lecture Hall

At 6:46 p.m. today, Oct. 17, a UCSC student reported that 30 minutes earlier, an unknown male jumped around the corner of a building near Thimann Lecture Hall and brandished a knife with an approximately 5-7-inch blade. Nothing was demanded of the student, and the student was not physically injured. The suspect was described as a clean-shaven White male, possibly in his 20s, with a medium build. He was wearing a black-hooded sweatshirt with an indistinguishable white logo on the left chest area and black jeans. If you have any information that might be helpful in this investigation, please contact Detective Frank Sanchez at fransanc@ucsc.edu, or, if you want, provide information through the UCPD Tip Line at 831-459-3847. Your information can be kept confidential.
